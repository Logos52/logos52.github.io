# Grok - MG and Kolbs Template

This synthesis covers the original MG & Kolbs Notion template (from the five Clippings videos + the exported ZIP) and compares it to the Obsidian version we are building.

## Core Thesis

The original MG & Kolbs Notion template is a **lean, reflection-driven personal operating system** built around five core databases (Tasks, Kolbs, Skills, Goal Tracking, Goals). Unlike broader “Command Center” templates, it deliberately focuses on **Marginal Gains + Kolb’s Experiential Learning Cycle**, with heavy Notion automation and a strong emphasis on process dissection, regular evaluation, and skill development. The Obsidian adaptation keeps the philosophical core while trading automation for deeper, more flexible reflection tools.

## Key Takeaways

- The template is intentionally narrow: it centers on five interconnected databases rather than trying to be a complete life system.
- The main “MG & Kolb’s Template” page is a simple hub — mostly links to the five databases plus the creator’s practical usage notes.
- **Kolbs** is the heart of the system. A purple checkbox button automates cycling between reflection entries.
- **Skills** is treated as a first-class database. Each skill tracks Current Level, Final Level, Competency stage, and “Final Level Metrics” (a checklist of concrete actions).
- **Goals** follow a detailed path: General Direction → Anchored Goal → Dissected Processes → Performance Goals → 1% Gains.
- The system relies heavily on **Notion automation** (template buttons, auto-created pages, calendar integration for rest timing).
- In Obsidian we gain better individual skill pages and more flexible reflection, but we lose the seamless daily execution automation that makes the Notion version feel effortless.

## Structure of the Original Notion Template

From the five Clippings and the exported workspace, the template is organized as follows:

**Main Hub Page**
- A simple dashboard that links to the five core databases: Tasks, Kolbs, Skills, Goal Tracking, and Goals.
- Contains the author’s personal usage notes (Notion Calendar integration, auto-created pages, the purple Kolbs cycling button, etc.).
- A few “Untitled” database views at the bottom (example or recent activity).

**Core Databases**

- **Goals** — Main goals with Anchored Goals, Dissected Processes, Performance Goals, and 1% Gains tracking.
- **Goal Tracking** — Weekly evaluations, 1% gains reviews, and progress logging.
- **Kolbs** — Structured reflection using the Kolb’s Cycle. A button automates creating and opening the next cycle.
- **Tasks** — Daily execution with prioritization and time-blocking via Notion Calendar.
- **Skills** — Tracks the specific skills, attributes, and mindsets being developed. Each skill has Current Level, Final Level, Competency stage, and Final Level Metrics (a checklist of what must be achieved).

**Key Automation Features**
- Purple ☑️ button in Kolbs that completes the current entry and opens the next one.
- Auto-created pages via database templates (especially in Kolbs and Goal Tracking).
- Notion Calendar integration for time-blocking and generating rest blocks.

## MG & Kolbs in Obsidian vs the Original Notion Template

**What We Are Keeping / Strengthening**
- The philosophical core: **Marginal Gains + Kolb’s Cycle** as the primary engine for improvement.
- Strong process dissection of goals.
- Individual skill development pages with Current Level, Final Level, and Final Level Metrics.
- Regular evaluation and 1% gains tracking.
- Clean separation between strategic (Goals) and reflective (Kolbs) layers.

**What Is Different / Improved in Obsidian**
- Individual skill pages (`mg-kolbs/Skills/SIR.md`, etc.) are more flexible and can contain richer notes and relations.
- The main `mg-kolbs/Skills.md` acts as a true dashboard with a live Dataview table.
- We have added supporting pages that make the system more robust (Weekly Review, Priority 0 Review, Experiments, Focus Training, Accountability & Mentors).
- Better cross-linking between pages (Goals ↔ Skills ↔ Kolbs).

**What Is Currently Missing or Lighter**
- The heavy Notion automation (purple buttons, auto-created pages, calendar-driven rest timing).
- A strong daily execution layer (Eisenhower Matrix, structured @Today page, Office Breaks system).
- Dedicated infrastructure for the Learning Zone (Syllabus tracking, Generated Questions Bank, Question Papers).
- Seamless 30 Day Plan system as a major tactical layer.
- Explicit accountability board and mentor outreach tracking.

## How to Use the MG & Kolbs Version (Obsidian)

Recommended daily/weekly flow:

1. **Morning / Planning**
   - Use **Tasks** for daily execution.
   - Reference your current **30 Day Plan** (even if lightweight).

2. **During the Day**
   - Do the work.
   - Log reflections in **Kolbs** after significant sessions or experiments.

3. **End of Week**
   - Run a **Weekly Review** or **Priority 0 Review**.
   - Update **Goal Tracking** (weekly evaluation + 1% gains).
   - Review and update **Skills** (current levels and metrics).

4. **Ongoing**
   - Run **Experiments** when testing new approaches.
   - Maintain **Accountability & Mentors**.
   - Use **Focus Training** and **1% Gains Log** for deliberate practice.

The system works best when you treat **reflection (Kolbs + Evaluation)** as the primary engine and execution (Tasks + 30 Day Plan) as the supporting layer.

## Things to Think About

- **Automation vs Friction**: The original Notion version feels effortless because of heavy automation. In Obsidian you will need to decide how much friction you are willing to accept in exchange for more flexible and private reflection practices.
- **Scope**: The MG & Kolbs philosophy is intentionally narrower than a full “Command Center.” If you need strong daily execution or academic study infrastructure, you may need to add those layers back.
- **Public vs Private**: Since parts of this system are being published, be very deliberate about what lives in the public `mg-kolbs/` folder versus the private `mg-kolbs/private/` folder.
- **Evolution**: The current Obsidian version is already more reflection-focused than the original Notion template. This is a strength, but it means you are evolving the system rather than copying it 1:1.
