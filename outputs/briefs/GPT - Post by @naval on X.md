# GPT - Post by @naval on X

## Core Thesis

Naval's tweetstorm argues that wealth is built through ownership, leverage, specific knowledge, accountability, and long-term games. The point is not to chase money or status directly. The point is to create assets, products, media, code, or businesses that compound beyond rented labor.

## Compressed Takeaways

1. **Seek wealth, not money or status.** Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep.
2. **Avoid renting all your time.** Labor alone does not scale.
3. **Acquire specific knowledge.** The most valuable knowledge is hard to train for and often tied to curiosity.
4. **Use leverage.** Code, media, capital, labor, and products multiply judgment.
5. **Take accountability.** Ownership requires visible risk and responsibility.
6. **Play long-term games.** Compounding relationships and reputation matter.
7. **Learn to sell and build.** Either is valuable; both together are powerful.
8. **Productize yourself.** Combine authenticity, specific knowledge, leverage, and accountability.

## System View

The tweetstorm is a compact operating philosophy:

- build specific knowledge,
- choose a domain where that knowledge matters,
- take accountability,
- apply leverage,
- play with long-term people,
- avoid status games,
- own assets.

## Relevance to This Knowledge Base

The most relevant overlap is with learning and agentic work:

- specific knowledge requires self-directed learning,
- leverage now includes AI agents and software,
- accountability matters more when output is cheap,
- judgment becomes more valuable as tools scale execution.
