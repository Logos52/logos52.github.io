# GPT - How to Change Your Life in 2026 with Reverse Goal Setting

## Core Thesis

Reverse goal setting starts from the desired future state and works backward to identify the systems, constraints, milestones, and behaviors required to reach it. The method is useful for complex goals because forward planning often gets trapped in today's habits and assumptions.

## Compressed Takeaways

1. **Start with the future state.** Define what the changed life or achieved goal looks like.
2. **Work backward.** Identify what must be true before that outcome can happen.
3. **Turn goals into systems.** The outcome is not enough; the process must be specified.
4. **Find constraints.** Ask what currently prevents the desired future.
5. **Design milestones.** Use intermediate states to make progress visible.
6. **Update the plan as reality responds.** Reverse planning is not static prediction.

## Practical Workflow

1. Name the desired future state.
2. Describe what life looks like when the goal is already achieved.
3. Identify the final precondition before success.
4. Keep stepping backward through necessary preconditions.
5. Convert preconditions into projects, habits, and decisions.
6. Identify the first action that starts the chain.
7. Review regularly and update the model.

This aligns with [[wiki/Techniques/Reverse Goal Setting|Reverse Goal Setting]] and [[wiki/Dimensions/Self-Management|Self-Management]].

## Why It Works

Forward goal setting often starts with current motivation. Reverse goal setting starts with required structure. That makes it easier to see missing capabilities, environmental constraints, and dependencies.

It is especially useful when:

- the goal is complex,
- progress is nonlinear,
- motivation fluctuates,
- multiple skills are involved,
- the learner needs a system rather than a wish.

## What This Should Feel Like

It should feel like turning a vague ambition into a chain of requirements.

It should not feel like writing affirmations, listing desires, or creating a plan that ignores constraints.
