# GPT - How to Think So Clearly People Assume You're A Genius

## Core Thesis

The source frames clear thinking as the removal of common thinking traps. The goal is not to sound smart. It is to make the structure of thought clean enough that problems, assumptions, options, and relationships become easier to see.

## Compressed Takeaways

1. **Clarity comes from structure.** Clear thinkers organize the problem before generating answers.
2. **Thinking traps create false confidence.** People often mistake fluency for understanding.
3. **Questions drive better thought.** The right question changes what the mind searches for.
4. **Externalization helps.** Thinking on paper or in maps exposes gaps.
5. **Metacognition improves accuracy.** Notice how you are thinking, not only what you conclude.
6. **Clear thinking is trainable.** It improves through repeated diagnosis and correction.

## Practical Workflow

1. State the problem plainly.
2. Identify what kind of problem it is.
3. Separate facts, assumptions, interpretations, and goals.
4. Ask what would change your mind.
5. Map the relationships.
6. Decide the next test or action.

This connects to [[wiki/Techniques/Thinking on Paper|Thinking on Paper]] and [[wiki/Red Team/Applied Critical Thinking|Applied Critical Thinking]].

## What This Should Feel Like

It should feel slower at the beginning and faster later. The early effort goes into framing. Once the frame is clean, decisions become easier.

It should not feel like generating impressive language around a confused model.
