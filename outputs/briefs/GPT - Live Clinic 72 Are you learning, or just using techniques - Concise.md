# GPT - Live Clinic 72: Are you learning, or just using techniques? - Concise

## Core Idea

Live Clinic 72 is about the difference between **doing a technique** and **learning**.

A technique is not the source of the result. The technique is only useful if it triggers the thinking that creates the result.

The important chain is:

> technique -> thinking -> knowledge -> performance

If the technique does not produce the right thinking, the chain breaks. The notes may look right, but the learner may still not be improving.

## The Main Mistake

The weak question is:

> Am I doing the technique correctly?

The better question is:

> Is this technique making me think at the level the result requires?

That shift is the whole clinic.

Technique ratings are misleading because no method is good in isolation. A method is good only if it fits the goal, the learner, the surrounding system, and the type of thinking needed.

## What Techniques Are For

Techniques are prompts for cognition.

Mind mapping is supposed to trigger organization and relationship-building. Retrieval is supposed to trigger reconstruction and discrimination. Interleaving is supposed to force comparison. BHS is supposed to build schema, not prettier notes.

The question is not whether the artifact exists. The question is whether the artifact came from the right thinking.

## Higher-Order Goals

Most serious learning goals are not just definitions or isolated recall. They require comparison, evaluation, application, organization, transfer, or problem-solving.

If the goal is higher-order but the technique is being used in a lower-order way, results will plateau. This is why someone can use "good" techniques and still not get better.

## The Shortcut Problem

The clinic's sharpest point is that the brain tries to escape hard thinking.

When the learner feels uncertainty or cognitive load, the brain looks for a simpler route: copy the answer, draw the arrow without explaining it, highlight without evaluating, ask someone else for the structure, make the map clean instead of meaningful.

These shortcuts reduce discomfort, but they also remove the learning.

So a good technique has to be hard to fake. It should force the learner to actually compare, evaluate, organize, or reconstruct.

## Subjective Signals

The clinic gives subjective feeling more importance than I initially gave it.

Daily self-correction often starts with asking whether this feels like the right kind of thinking. Is curiosity increasing? Are questions appearing more freely? Does the topic feel more organized? Can I move through the structure more comfortably?

These feelings are not proof of mastery. But they are useful steering signals before objective performance data exists.

## Misread Effort

A learner can abandon a useful method too early because the improvement is subtle, the method feels hard, or the execution skill is still weak.

So when results do not improve, do not immediately blame the technique. Ask whether the technique matched the goal, whether it triggered the intended thinking, whether you shortcut the hard part, or whether you are still too new at the method to feel progress yet.

## Essence

Clinic 72 is the technique-level diagnostic:

> The method only matters if it produces the cognition the goal requires.

The target feeling is organized usability. The topic should start to feel less confusing, more connected, and more usable. Not perfectly memorized. Usable.

That is the difference between technique use and learning.
