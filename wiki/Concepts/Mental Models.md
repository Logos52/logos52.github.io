# Mental Models

Mental models are simplified representations of how the world (or specific domains) works. They help with prediction, decision-making, and understanding by compressing complex reality into usable patterns.

A good mental model lets you reason about situations you haven't directly experienced.

## Core Thesis

The quality of your thinking is heavily determined by the quality and diversity of your mental models. Having multiple models from different disciplines allows you to see problems from several angles and avoid the blind spots of any single framework.

## Practical Use

- When facing a difficult problem, deliberately apply 2–3 different mental models.
- Collect models that are high-leverage and widely applicable.
- Be wary of over-applying a single favorite model (the "law of the instrument").
