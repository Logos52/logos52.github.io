# Game Theory

The mathematical study of strategic interaction between rational agents who are trying to maximize their own outcomes.

## Core Thesis

Many social, economic, and competitive situations can be modeled as games where the choices of one player affect the payoffs of others. Understanding the structure of these interactions (payoffs, information, repeated vs one-shot) helps predict behavior and design better strategies.

## Key Concepts

**Zero-Sum Games**  
One player's gain is exactly another player's loss. Total payoff is fixed. Common in direct competition.

**Positive-Sum (Win-Win) Games**  
Cooperation or exchange can increase the total value available to all parties.

**Nash Equilibrium**  
A state where no player can improve their outcome by unilaterally changing strategy, assuming others stay the same.

**Repeated Games and Trust**  
In repeated interactions, reputation, punishment, and future consequences change optimal strategies dramatically (see *The Evolution of Trust*).

## Practical Applications

- Negotiation and bargaining
- Pricing and market competition
- Social dilemmas and cooperation
- Evolutionary biology and signaling
- Personal strategy and decision making
