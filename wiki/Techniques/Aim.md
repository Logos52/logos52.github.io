# Aim

Aim is the first step of [[wiki/Techniques/Bear Hunter System|Bear Hunter System]]. It directs attention toward high-yield relationships before the learner gets buried in details.

## Summary

Aim is a question-generation and orientation step. Its job is to make the learner curious about structure, importance, and relationships.

The learner is not trying to answer everything yet. The learner is deciding what would be worth understanding.

## Cognitive Job

Aim should activate:

- Purpose.
- Curiosity.
- Importance judgment.
- Relationship search.
- Big-picture orientation.
- Initial chunk hypotheses.

## Good Aim Questions

- Why does this matter?
- What problem does this solve?
- What does this influence?
- What depends on this?
- How does this relate to the topic's main goal?
- What would be confusing if this concept were removed?

## Output

Aim should usually produce:

- A small set of high-yield questions.
- A rough concept list.
- Tentative chunks.
- A sense of what relationships to look for during [[wiki/Techniques/Shoot|Shoot]].
