# Self-Awareness and Reflection

Self-Awareness and Reflection is the Red Team principle that starts with the decision maker's own mind: temperament, emotion, values, needs, memories, biases, and identity.

## Core Idea

A Red Teamer cannot challenge a plan honestly while remaining blind to their own frame. The handbook treats self-awareness as the first protection against bad decisions because every perception is filtered through a person.

Self-awareness asks:

- What am I assuming because it is familiar?
- What emotional reaction am I having?
- What personal need or value is being activated?
- What kind of evidence do I tend to overtrust?
- What kind of person or idea do I dismiss too quickly?
- What parts of my story make this issue feel obvious?

## Self-Authorship

The handbook uses self-authorship as a developmental frame. The goal is to build an internal compass strong enough to handle ambiguity, disagreement, and new information without simply absorbing the surrounding group's beliefs.

This connects directly to [[wiki/Dimensions/Mindset|Mindset]]. A person with weak self-authorship is easily pulled by approval, rank, tribe, or fear of looking wrong. A person with stronger self-authorship can stay open to evidence while retaining independent judgment.

## Reflection Practices

The handbook emphasizes introspection, daily journaling, and "Who Am I?" style self-examination. The point is not diary-keeping as sentiment. The point is to inspect the machinery of thought.

Useful prompts:

- What did I react to strongly today?
- Where did I feel defensive?
- What did I avoid saying?
- What perspective did I not want to consider?
- What did I learn about my own decision pattern?
- Where did my temperament help, and where did it distort?
