# Perspective-Shifting Tools

Perspective-shifting tools force the thinker to leave the default frame and inspect the situation through another actor, culture, incentive structure, or future.

## Why They Matter

Plans fail when decision makers assume the world looks the same to everyone. Red Teaming treats viewpoint as part of the terrain.

## High-Value Tools

- [[wiki/Red Team/Four Ways of Seeing|Four Ways of Seeing]]
- [[wiki/Red Team/Stakeholder Mapping|Stakeholder Mapping]]
- Cultural perception frameworks
- Alternative futures analysis
- Outside-in thinking

## Core Questions

- How do we see ourselves?
- How does the other actor see us?
- How do we see them?
- How do they see themselves?
- What does this plan signal?
- What incentives does the other actor actually face?
- What would make their behavior rational from their frame?

## Personal Use

Perspective shifting is useful for:

- Language learning: see meaning from inside the target language instead of translating.
- Learning: see the topic from the examiner, practitioner, beginner, and expert frames.
- AI work: see the output from user, maintainer, adversary, and future-self perspectives.
