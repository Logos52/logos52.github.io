# Analysis of Competing Hypotheses

Analysis of Competing Hypotheses is a structured way to compare possible explanations against evidence, with special attention to evidence that disproves or weakens a hypothesis.

## Core Idea

People naturally look for evidence that supports the explanation they already like. ACH reverses the pressure. It asks which hypothesis survives the evidence best.

## Basic Method

1. List plausible hypotheses.
2. List relevant evidence and observations.
3. Compare each piece of evidence against each hypothesis.
4. Mark whether the evidence is consistent, inconsistent, or not useful for each hypothesis.
5. Pay special attention to inconsistent evidence.
6. Prefer the hypothesis with the fewest serious contradictions, not the one with the most attractive supporting details.

## Why It Matters

ACH is a [[wiki/Dimensions/Deep Processing|Deep Processing]] tool because it forces comparison. It also protects [[wiki/Dimensions/Mindset|Mindset]] by making disagreement less personal: the matrix, not ego, carries the argument.

## Personal Translation

Use ACH when:

- Explaining why a study method failed.
- Diagnosing a language-learning plateau.
- Evaluating competing interpretations of a source.
- Reviewing an LLM answer that could be wrong in multiple ways.
- Deciding between several project strategies.
