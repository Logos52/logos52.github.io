# Four Principles of Red Teaming

The Red Team Handbook organizes UFMCS Red Teaming around four interlaced principles: self-awareness, groupthink mitigation, cultural empathy, and applied critical thinking.

## The Four Principles

| Principle | Core Question | Failure It Counters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [[wiki/Red Team/Self-Awareness and Reflection|Self-Awareness and Reflection]] | What in me is shaping how I see this? | Unexamined bias, emotion, temperament, identity, and habit. |
| [[wiki/Red Team/Groupthink Mitigation and Decision Support|Groupthink Mitigation and Decision Support]] | What is the group suppressing or prematurely agreeing on? | Hierarchy, conformity, silence, premature consensus. |
| [[wiki/Red Team/Fostering Cultural Empathy|Fostering Cultural Empathy]] | How does this look from inside another actor's world? | Mirror imaging, ethnocentrism, stereotyping, shallow cultural models. |
| [[wiki/Red Team/Applied Critical Thinking|Applied Critical Thinking]] | What assumptions, arguments, analogies, and alternatives need testing? | Cognitive autopilot, weak logic, bad analogies, untested claims. |

## How They Work Together

Self-awareness starts with the individual. If the thinker cannot notice their own frames, they will mistake their first interpretation for reality.

Groupthink mitigation moves from individual to team. It protects the decision process from rank, personality, conformity, and premature closure.

Cultural empathy moves outside the team. It asks how other actors define meaning, status, threat, opportunity, and acceptable behavior.

Applied critical thinking gives the methods. It turns suspicion into a disciplined process: ask better questions, deconstruct arguments, test assumptions, and explore alternatives.
