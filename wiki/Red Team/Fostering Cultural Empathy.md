# Fostering Cultural Empathy

Fostering Cultural Empathy is the Red Team principle of understanding how other actors perceive, interpret, value, and act from within their own cultural and situational frame.

## Core Idea

Cultural empathy is not sympathy, agreement, or moral approval. It is disciplined perspective-taking. The Red Teamer tries to understand why an actor's behavior makes sense to that actor.

This counters mirror imaging: assuming others want, fear, value, and interpret the same way we do.

## What To Look For

- Local meanings of honor, shame, duty, security, status, family, religion, money, and authority.
- Formal and informal power structures.
- Economic rhythms and constraints.
- Kinship, age, gender, class, ethnicity, and group identity.
- Historical memory and grievance.
- What an actor believes they must protect.
- What an actor believes others are trying to do to them.

## Red Team Use

Fostering cultural empathy helps the team ask:

- What are we assuming about the other actor?
- What would this plan signal to them?
- What behavior looks irrational only because our frame is wrong?
- What incentives are invisible from our side?
- Which stakeholders are being treated as generic categories instead of real actors?
