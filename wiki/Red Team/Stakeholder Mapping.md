# Stakeholder Mapping

Stakeholder Mapping systematically identifies who can affect or be affected by a decision, then analyzes their interests, influence, perception, and likely behavior.

## Core Idea

Plans fail when they treat "people" as a generic block. Stakeholder Mapping forces the planner to name actors and distinguish interests, power, support, opposition, and change over time.

## Method

1. List stakeholders broadly, including unattended or overlooked actors.
2. Analyze interests, not just stated positions.
3. Assess power and influence.
4. Map support/opposition against influence.
5. Identify who needs engagement, monitoring, coalition-building, or mitigation.
6. Revisit the map as stakeholders change.

## Cautions

- The map is subjective.
- Stakeholders change.
- Influence can matter more than loud opposition.
- Quiet actors may become decisive later.
- Your team may misunderstand other actors' interests.

## Personal Use

Use it for:

- Product decisions.
- Writing public notes.
- Studying with teachers, peers, or online communities.
- Understanding cultural contexts in language learning.
- Planning how to communicate a new idea.
