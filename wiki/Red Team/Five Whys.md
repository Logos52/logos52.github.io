# Five Whys

Five Whys is a root-cause questioning tool. It repeatedly asks why a problem exists until the analysis moves past symptoms.

## Core Idea

The first explanation is often a symptom. Repeated why-questions push the thinker toward causes, incentives, dependencies, and purpose.

## Method

1. State the issue.
2. Ask why it happened or why it matters.
3. Answer plainly.
4. Ask why again.
5. Continue until the answer reveals a deeper cause, a loop, or a better question.
6. If the answers start circling, switch to a how-question.

## Personal Use

Use it when:

- A study session failed.
- Motivation dropped.
- A project stalled.
- An LLM answer feels off.
- A plan has a symptom but no diagnosis.
