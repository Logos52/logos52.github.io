# Four Ways of Seeing

Four Ways of Seeing is a perspective-shifting tool for escaping one-sided analysis.

## The Four Views

Ask:

1. How do we see ourselves?
2. How do we see them?
3. How do they see us?
4. How do they see themselves?

## Why It Works

Most plans are built from the planner's view of the world. Four Ways of Seeing forces the team to treat perception as terrain. It is especially useful when dealing with adversaries, partners, customers, cultures, or anyone whose incentives differ from yours.

## Personal Use

Use it when:

- A plan depends on another person's reaction.
- You are interpreting conflict.
- A language or cultural behavior feels irrational.
- You are designing for users unlike yourself.
- You are evaluating how your work will be received.
