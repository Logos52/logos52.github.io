# Devils Advocacy

Devils Advocacy is the deliberate construction of a contrary argument to test the preferred view.

## Core Idea

The point is not to be negative. The point is to make dissent legitimate before the decision is locked in.

## Use When

- The team is converging too quickly.
- The leader's preference is dominating.
- The plan has not faced serious opposition.
- Everyone agrees but the stakes are high.
- An LLM output sounds persuasive but has not been stress tested.

## Method

1. State the preferred conclusion.
2. Assign someone or an LLM to argue against it.
3. Require specific evidence, not vague negativity.
4. Identify which objections are serious.
5. Modify the plan or explain why the objection does not hold.

## Personal Use

Ask:

- What is the strongest argument against my current interpretation?
- What would a competent critic say?
- What am I not allowed to notice because I want this to work?
- If this is wrong, what kind of wrong is it?
