# Team A Team B Analysis

Team A / Team B Analysis separates competing views into structured cases so decision makers can compare strong arguments rather than blurred compromise.

## Core Idea

When a community has two serious competing interpretations, the disagreement should be clarified, not hidden. Separate teams build the best case for each view, including evidence, assumptions, and implications.

## Use When

- Experts disagree on an important issue.
- A decision is high stakes.
- A consensus report would hide real uncertainty.
- The group needs to understand why reasonable people disagree.

## Personal Adaptation

For solo work:

1. Write Team A: the strongest case for your current view.
2. Write Team B: the strongest case against it.
3. Compare assumptions.
4. Compare evidence quality.
5. Identify what new evidence would decide the issue.
