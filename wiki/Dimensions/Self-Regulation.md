# Self-Regulation

Self-Regulation is the dimension responsible for monitoring, diagnosing, and adjusting the learning process.

Red Teaming is a professionalized form of self-regulation for decisions. It asks whether the current frame, group process, assumptions, and failure analysis are good enough before committing to action.

## Summary

Self-Regulation is the control system. It notices what is happening during learning, interprets the signal, and chooses the next adjustment.

If [[wiki/Dimensions/Deep Processing|Deep Processing]] is the encoding engine and [[wiki/Dimensions/Retrieval|Retrieval]] is the testing engine, Self-Regulation is the driver.

The strongest recent refinement is the pair of [[wiki/Syntheses/First Principles of ICS|First Principles of ICS]] and [[wiki/Syntheses/Are You Learning, or Just Using Techniques|Are You Learning, or Just Using Techniques]]. The first treats overwhelm as the cue for meta-strategy. The second checks whether a technique is actually triggering the thinking it was supposed to trigger.

## What This Dimension Controls

- Whether the learner notices passive drift.
- Whether difficulty is interpreted correctly.
- Whether gaps are diagnosed at the right level.
- Whether techniques are applied for the right reason.
- Whether the learner can adapt without needing constant external guidance.

## Core Loop

1. Set a learning goal.
2. Choose a method.
3. Monitor the cognitive process while using it.
4. Check the result.
5. Diagnose what worked or failed.
6. Modify the next attempt.

## How It Connects BHS And SIR

BHS and SIR both require Self-Regulation.

During [[wiki/Techniques/Bear Hunter System|Bear Hunter System]], Self-Regulation checks:

- Am I asking questions that force relationships?
- Am I actually judging importance?
- Is my map reducing cognitive load or adding decoration?
- Is confusion productive or a sign that the scope is too large?

During [[wiki/Techniques/Spaced Interleaved Retrieval|Spaced Interleaved Retrieval]], Self-Regulation checks:

- Did I retrieve or merely recognize?
- What level of mastery failed?
- Should this gap be repaired by retrieval, re-encoding, or procedural practice?
- Is the next retrieval session timed and varied well enough?

## Relationship To Metacognition

[[wiki/Concepts/Metacognition - The Control Layer|Metacognition: The Control Layer]] is awareness of thinking. Self-Regulation uses that awareness to steer behavior.

Metacognition notices. Self-Regulation acts.

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Signal | Repair |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Technique theater | The learner performs the steps without the intended cognition. | Name the cognitive job of the step before doing it. |
| Outcome-only reflection | Success is judged only by grades, pages, or time spent. | Reflect on process signals and gap types. |
| Misread difficulty | Useful strain is treated as failure. | Compare effort against learning output and retrieval results. |
| Over-adjustment | Constantly switching methods before skill develops. | Hold one variable stable long enough to learn from it. |
| Under-adjustment | Repeating the same ineffective routine. | Run a short diagnostic and change one variable. |
