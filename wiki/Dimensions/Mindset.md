# Mindset

Mindset is the dimension responsible for how the learner interprets difficulty, mistakes, feedback, identity, and growth.

Two book notes now feed this hub from different angles. [[wiki/Books/The Parasitic Mind|The Parasitic Mind]] is useful for epistemic courage: noticing when identity, tribe, or fear of offense distorts truth-seeking. [[wiki/Books/The Book of Elon|The Book of Elon]] is useful for builder courage: choosing usefulness, constraints, and action over comfort or commentary.

## Summary

Mindset is not positive thinking. It is the interpretive layer that determines whether difficulty becomes useful feedback or evidence of personal inadequacy.

In this system, Mindset supports sustained contact with challenge. It makes [[wiki/Dimensions/Deep Processing|Deep Processing]], [[wiki/Dimensions/Retrieval|Retrieval]], and [[wiki/Dimensions/Self-Regulation|Self-Regulation]] emotionally sustainable.

## What This Dimension Controls

- Response to mistakes.
- Willingness to attempt difficult tasks.
- Openness to feedback.
- Whether identity becomes fixed around performance.
- Whether effort is interpreted as growth or threat.
- Whether the learner stays process-oriented under pressure.

## Core Moves

- Treat mistakes as information.
- Separate performance from identity.
- Define progress by skill development, not only outcomes.
- Make small, frequent, survivable attempts.
- Reflect on what changed, not just whether the result was good.
- Seek feedback without turning it into self-judgment.

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Signal | Repair |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fixed identity | "I am bad at this" replaces diagnosis. | Reframe as a process gap or skill gap. |
| Challenge avoidance | Easy tasks crowd out high-value hard tasks. | Lower the consequence of the first attempt. |
| Feedback threat | Corrections feel like attacks. | Separate data from identity. |
| Outcome fixation | Grades/results become the only progress signal. | Track process quality and marginal gains. |
| Perfectionism | Starting is delayed until certainty is high. | Use small attempts with fast feedback. |
