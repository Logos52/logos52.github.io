# 30-Day Challenge – Self-Management

## Week 1: Auditing Current Systems

Review existing routines, environments, and task structures. Identify what is currently protecting focus and what is creating friction or leakage. Make a simple inventory of what is working and what is not.

## Week 2: Protecting Time and Energy

Design or adjust one system that creates protected time or energy. This could involve scheduling, environment setup, or reducing decision fatigue. Test the change for consistency over the week.

## Week 3: Reducing Start Friction

Focus on lowering the activation energy required to begin learning sessions. Experiment with preparation habits, default setups, or environmental cues that make starting easier and more automatic.

## Week 4: Building Feedback Loops

Introduce simple ways to track whether your systems are actually working. This could include daily check-ins, weekly reviews, or visible progress markers. Adjust based on what the feedback reveals.
