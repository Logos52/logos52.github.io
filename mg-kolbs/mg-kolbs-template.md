# MG & Kolbs Template

## Core Thesis

The original Notion template is a broad **Command Center** that splits life into three zones: Action (daily execution), Progress (goal achievement through process thinking), and Learning (structured studying). The MG & Kolbs version is a narrowing of this idea — it keeps the reflection and continuous improvement core (Kolb’s Cycle + Marginal Gains + Process Dissection) while stripping away most of the daily execution and academic infrastructure in favor of a tighter, more personal operating layer.

## Key Takeaways

- The original template has three distinct zones: Action (daily execution), Progress (goals + 1% gains), and Learning (SIR + study systems).
- MG & Kolbs is essentially a **refined and focused Progress Zone** with strong Kolb’s Cycle integration.
- The original template is designed as a complete life operating system; MG & Kolbs is a more opinionated, reflection-heavy subset.
- Many powerful elements from the original (Eisenhower Matrix, Office Breaks, @Today page, Syllabus tracking, Question Banks) are currently missing or deprioritized in MG & Kolbs.
- The MG & Kolbs version works best when treated as a **thinking and improvement layer** rather than a full productivity suite.
- The original template relies heavily on Notion automations and buttons; MG & Kolbs requires more manual discipline in Obsidian.
- The name “MG & Kolbs” signals a clear philosophy (Marginal Gains + Kolb’s Cycle) rather than trying to be a generic all-in-one tool.

## Structure of the Original Notion Template

The template is organized around a **Command Center** with three zones:

**Action Zone**
- Eisenhower Matrix + Star prioritization
- Tasks and Mini-tasks (COBEs and Experiments)
- @Today page for daily planning and review
- Google Calendar time-blocking
- Office Breaks with automatic rest timing
- Focus training and attention journaling

**Progress Zone**
- Goals → Anchored Goals → Dissected Processes
- Performance Goals (SMARTER)
- 1% Gains
- 30 Day Plans
- Evaluations (weekly + process level)
- COBEs (experiments) on processes
- Accountability and mentorship tracking

**Learning Zone**
- SIR (Spaced Interleaved Retrieval)
- Syllabus tracking
- Generated Questions Bank
- Question Papers
- Subjects and study sessions

There is also a separate **Second Brain** area for documents and resources.

## MG & Kolbs vs the Full Template

**What MG & Kolbs Keeps (and Strengthens)**
- Process dissection of goals
- Evaluation + 1% Gains loop
- Kolb’s Cycle as a primary reflection tool
- Skills development and tracking
- Marginal Gains as the central philosophy

**What Is Currently Weak or Missing**
- Strong daily execution system (Eisenhower Matrix, @Today page, Office Breaks)
- Robust experiment tracking as a first-class practice
- Dedicated Learning Zone infrastructure (Syllabus, Question Banks, generated questions)
- Structured 30 Day Plan system as a major tactical layer
- Explicit accountability board and mentor outreach
- Focus training and attention journaling tools

The MG & Kolbs version is more focused and easier to maintain, but it gives up a lot of the “complete operating system” feeling of the original.

## How to Use the MG & Kolbs Version

Treat it as a **reflection and improvement operating layer**, not a full productivity tool.

Recommended flow:
- Use **Goals → Anchored Goals → Dissected Processes** to define direction
- Run regular **Evaluation** cycles (weekly or per process) to rate progress and extract 1% gains
- Use **Kolbs** as your main reflection engine after significant work or experiments
- Use **Tasks** and **30 Day Plan** for short-term execution (currently lighter)
- Track developing capabilities in the **Skills** database

It shines when your primary goal is honest, structured self-improvement rather than maximum daily output or heavy academic studying.

## Things to Think About

- **Scope vs Depth**: The original template tries to be a complete life system. MG & Kolbs trades breadth for depth in reflection and marginal gains. Decide which approach actually serves you.
- **Execution Layer**: If daily action and time management are important, you will likely need to add (or keep) elements from the Action Zone that are currently missing.
- **Study System**: If you do high-volume academic work, you may eventually want to bring back parts of the Learning Zone (SIR infrastructure, question generation, syllabus tracking).
- **Hybrid Use**: Many people run MG & Kolbs in Obsidian for reflection and thinking, while handling execution (calendar, daily tasks) in another tool.

## Open Questions

- How much of the original template’s automation do you want to replicate in Obsidian?
- Is MG & Kolbs meant to eventually become your full operating system, or will it remain a focused improvement + reflection layer alongside other tools?
